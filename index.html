Shiba Inu Tribute Page

Overview

Shiba Inu are a small-to-medium sized breed of hunting dog originating from Japan. The Shiba Inu dog is small, alert and agile. They are able to cope with mountainous terrain due to having doubled coated fur. Shiba Inu are intelligent however for the inexperienced dog owner they can be diffcult to train. This is because they display an independant nature and are generally not food motivated.

Health

Overall the Shiba Inu breed is a healthly dog breed. Eye tests and periodic joint examinations are recommended. By the age of two Shiba Inu's may be considered free of joint problems if none have been discovered. This is because at the age of two their Skeleton is fully developed. Shiba Inu require daily exercise and their life-expectancy is between 11-16 years. The oldest Shiba Inu was Pusuke who died aged 26.

History

The Shiba Inu predates the modern Dog breeds of the 19th century. Shiba Inu were breed to hunt small Birds and Rabbits and to flush game. From 1912 to 1926 the number of pure blood Shiba Inu decreased and by World War 2 the breed was in danger of dying out. All subsequent dogs were breed from the only three surviving bloodlines.
